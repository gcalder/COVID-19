<h2 id="overall-update">Overall update</h2>
<p>Here is the daily report for COVID-19 in Scotland for Friday, 17 April 2020. For the full dashboard, including comparisons with other countries, please visit <a href="https://smazeri.shinyapps.io/Covid19_Scotland">here</a>.</p>
<p><strong>Deaths:</strong><br />
The death toll is currently 837.<br />
The number of new deaths reported today was 58, which represents a 7.4% increase on yesterday’s total.<br />
The average daily growth rate for new deaths measured over the last 7 days was +7.9%, which is significantly greater than 0.</p>
<p><strong>Cases:</strong><br />
The total number of cases is currently 7,409.<br />
The number of new cases today is 307, which represents a 4.3% increase on yesterday’s total.<br />
The average daily growth rate for new cases measured over the last 7 days was +3.8%, which is not significantly greater than 0.</p>
<p><strong>Cases per capita (measured per 10,000 head of population):</strong><br />
The highest incidence over the last day was 1.1, which occurred in Fife.<br />
The highest cumulative incidence is 23.5, which is in Tayside.</p>
<p><strong>Health Boards*:</strong><br />
The largest increase in cases in absolute terms by health board was 65 cases, which occurred in Lothian.<br />
The largest relative increase in cases was 9.4%, which occurred in Fife.<br />
The fastest average daily growth rate for new cases over the past 7 days was +6.4%, which is significantly greater than 0 and occurred in Ayrshire.</p>
<p><strong>Tests:</strong><br />
The total number of tests reported is 36,637.<br />
The total number of positive cases to date is 7,409.<br />
The percentage of tests which were positive is currently 20.2%.</p>
<p>*To enhance graph readability and to account for cases being reallocated across health boards, the following health boards are grouped together: Grampian, Shetland and Orkney; Highland and Western Isles.</p>
