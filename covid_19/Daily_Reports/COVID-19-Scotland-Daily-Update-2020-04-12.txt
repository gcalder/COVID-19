<p>Here is the daily report for COVID-19 in Scotland for Sunday, 12 April 2020. For the full dashboard, including comparisons with other countries, please visit <a href="https://smazeri.shinyapps.io/Covid19_Scotland">here</a>.</p>
<p><strong>Deaths:</strong><br />
The death toll is currently 566.<br />
The number of new deaths reported today was 24, which represents a 4.4% increase on yesterday’s total.<br />
The doubling time for deaths over the past 7 days was 5.1 days.</p>
<p><strong>Cases:</strong><br />
The total number of cases is currently 5,912.<br />
The number of new cases today is 322, which represents a 5.8% increase on yesterday’s total.<br />
The doubling time for the number of cases over the past 7 days was 10.4 days.</p>
<p><strong>Cases per capita (measured per 10,000 head of population):</strong><br />
The highest incidence over the last day was 1.2, which occurred in Tayside.<br />
The highest cumulative incidence is 19.3, which is in Tayside.</p>
<p><strong>Health Boards*:</strong><br />
The largest increase in cases in absolute terms by health board was 62 cases, which occurred in Greater Glasgow and Clyde.<br />
The largest relative increase in cases was 9.7%, which occurred in Grampian, Shetland and Orkney.<br />
The fastest doubling time over the past 7 days was 7.6 days, which occurred in Fife.</p>
<p><strong>Tests:</strong><br />
The total number of tests reported is 31,114.<br />
The total number of positive cases to date is 5,912.<br />
The percentage of tests which were positive is currently 19.0%.</p>
<p>*To enhance graph readability and to account for cases being reallocated across health boards, the following health boards are grouped together: Grampian, Shetland and Orkney; Highland and Western Isles.</p>
