<p>Here is the daily report for COVID-19 in Scotland for Tuesday, 14 April 2020. For the full dashboard, including comparisons with other countries, please visit <a href="https://smazeri.shinyapps.io/Covid19_Scotland">here</a>.</p>
<p><strong>Deaths:</strong><br />
The death toll is currently 615.<br />
The number of new deaths reported today was 40, which represents a 7.0% increase on yesterday’s total.<br />
The doubling time for deaths over the past 7 days was 6.6 days.</p>
<p><strong>Cases:</strong><br />
The total number of cases is currently 6,358.<br />
The number of new cases today is 291, which represents a 4.8% increase on yesterday’s total.<br />
The doubling time for the number of cases over the past 7 days was 11.9 days.</p>
<p><strong>Cases per capita (measured per 10,000 head of population):</strong><br />
The highest incidence over the last day was 0.8, which occurred in Greater Glasgow and Clyde.<br />
The highest cumulative incidence is 19.9, which is in Tayside.</p>
<p><strong>Health Boards*:</strong><br />
